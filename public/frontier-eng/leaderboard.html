Average rank

On each task, models are sorted by best feasible score (higher is better; ties split evenly) and given a rank. Average rank is the mean of those ranks across all tasks—lower is better. It compares models without mixing incompatible units (throughput, cost, optics metrics, etc.).

Performance profile

For each task, form a performance ratio: task-best score divided by this model’s best score (higher is better for the raw score, so a ratio of 1 means tied for best on that task). The curve plots the fraction of tasks where the ratio stays at or below the value on the horizontal axis (a tolerance); tolerance 1 is strict, larger values allow more slack. Curves that are higher toward the left mean stronger, more consistent coverage. Same idea as Dolan–Moré benchmarking on the frozen leaderboard scores.
